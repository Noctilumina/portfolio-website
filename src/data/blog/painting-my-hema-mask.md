# Painting My HEMA Mask

So I decided to paint my fencing mask. It turned out to be a really fun little project.

## Why Paint It?

Honestly? Everyone's mask looks the same. Black mesh, maybe some club colors if you're lucky. I wanted mine to stand out. Plus I'd seen some really cool custom painted masks online and thought "how hard can that be?" (I need to stop asking myself that question.)

## The Process

First things first: you need to prep the mesh properly. The metal needs to be clean and slightly roughed up so the paint actually sticks. I used some fine grit sandpaper to scuff the surface, then wiped everything down.

![Mask before painting](/images/blog/hema-mask-before.jpg)

For the paint I went with enamel spray paint. It's more durable than acrylic and handles the occasional sword hit better. You want to do thin coats. Multiple thin coats. I cannot stress this enough. If you go too thick the paint pools in the mesh holes and you end up with a bumpy mess.

![First coat of paint](/images/blog/hema-mask-first-coat.jpg)

The design itself took some planning. I taped off the areas I wanted to keep separate and worked in stages. Each color needed to fully dry before I could tape over it and do the next one.

![Taping off the design](/images/blog/hema-mask-taped.jpg)

## The Result

I'm honestly pretty happy with how it turned out. It's not perfect up close but from sparring distance it looks great. And more importantly, I can instantly spot my mask in a pile of identical black ones after practice.

![Finished mask](/images/blog/hema-mask-finished.jpg)

## Tips If You Want to Try This

- **Prep is everything.** Seriously. Clean and scuff the mesh or the paint will flake off after two sessions.
- **Thin coats.** Three thin coats beats one thick coat every time.
- **Enamel over acrylic.** It's tougher and handles impact better.
- **Let it cure fully.** Don't be impatient and take it to practice the next day. Give it at least a week to fully harden.
- **Accept imperfection.** Painting on mesh is weird. It won't look like painting on a flat surface. That's fine.

![Mask in action at practice](/images/blog/hema-mask-action.jpg)

## Was It Worth It?

Absolutely. It took a couple of evenings and cost maybe 15 euros in paint and tape. Now I have a mask that's actually mine, not just another identical black one. Plus it's a good conversation starter at tournaments.

Would I do it again? Already planning the next design.

# New in the CPR Wiki: Homebrew Rules, Explosives, and Night City Slang

Three new articles, a set of quickref cards, and a homebrew rules section.

## Iris Expansion

Iris is a set of homebrew rules I run in my campaigns. They cover mechanics that aren't in the core book or that I've replaced with versions that work better at my table: extended chase sequences, a social reputation track, NPC morale, and a few other gaps that kept coming up in play.

The wiki now has an **Iris Expansion** section with all of these documented. More usefully, the wiki now supports adding your own homebrew rules. You can write entries in the same format as the standard articles and they'll appear in search results and under a dedicated Homebrew category in the sidebar.

This isn't a collaborative platform — the wiki runs on static data files and isn't user-editable in the traditional sense. But if you want to adapt the format for your own campaign, the structure is documented and the homebrew category is ready for it.

## Explosives & Demolitions

The core book has rules for explosives spread across multiple chapters. Grenades are in equipment. The Demolitions skill is in skills. Shaped charge rules are in combat. This article pulls all of it into one place: every explosive type, the Demolitions skill check DV table, blast radius, fuse timing, and a step-by-step procedure for both combat use and non-combat demolitions (blowing a wall, cracking a vault).

## Street Slang & Abbreviations

Night City has its own vocabulary. New players hit "gonk," "choom," "edgerunner," "flatline," corpo acronyms, and gang terminology in the first session and either stop to ask or just let it go over their heads. This article is a full reference: Night City slang, common abbreviations, and gang terminology that comes up in play.

It's searchable and tagged, and links to relevant Lore & History articles for terms with more context behind them.

![Street slang article with Night City vocabulary entries](/images/blog/cpr-wiki-new-content/street-slang.png)

## Throwing Weapons Quickref

Two new procedure cards in the Quick Ref: throwing weapons and throwing grenades. Throwing in Cyberpunk Red has specific range rules and some non-obvious interactions with aimed shots that come up more often than you'd expect. The cards have the full step-by-step for each, with the key mechanics bolded the same way as every other quickref entry.

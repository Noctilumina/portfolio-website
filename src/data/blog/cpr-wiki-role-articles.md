# The CPR Wiki Now Has Actual Role Articles (Not Just Stat Blocks)

The Cyberpunk Red wiki launched with articles covering combat procedures, equipment, rules references, and brief role summaries. The summaries covered what each role does and what their special ability is. They didn't cover how to actually play one.

## What Changed

All ten roles now have full articles: Rockerboy, Solo, Netrunner, Tech, Medtech, Media, Exec, Lawman, Fixer, and Nomad.

Each article covers:

- **The character** — what kind of person plays this role, what they care about, where they fit in Night City
- **The special ability** — full mechanics including edge cases, not just a one-paragraph description
- **How the role plays at the table** — the decisions you make session to session, where the role shines, where it struggles
- **Examples** — concrete scenarios showing how the ability works in practice

![Netrunner role article showing the Interface ability and example scenarios](/images/blog/cpr-wiki-role-articles/netrunner-article.png)

## Why

Playing a Netrunner for the first time is genuinely confusing. The rules are scattered: some in the role section, more in the Netrunning chapter, more in the equipment catalog, more in the Black ICE entries. A new player has to piece it all together. The wiki article puts it in one place, in order, with examples showing what the options actually look like in play.

Same issue with Exec: the Teamwork ability is mechanically simple but it changes how your whole group approaches a session once you understand what it can enable. The article explains that.

![Fixer role article covering the Operator ability and contacts system](/images/blog/cpr-wiki-role-articles/fixer-article.png)

The Solo article is the most detailed. Combat Sense has a lot of moving parts and players consistently underuse it — partly because the full picture isn't obvious from reading the ability description alone.

## Linking

Role articles are under the **Roles** category in the wiki sidebar. Each one links to related quickref cards — if you're reading the Medtech article, there are direct links to the Stabilize and Surgery procedure cards. If you're reading Solo, there are links to every combat procedure the ability interacts with.
